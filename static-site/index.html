As a Kid

Like most thirteen year old boys, I had an abundance of time and energy, and I found a hobby that allowed me to utilize those resources. Unlike most thirteen year old boys, my hobby was teaching myself C. With no internet access and no IDE, I checked out a book from the library, C for Dummies, which came with a CD copy of the GNU command-line compiler. Over the summer, whenever I wasn't with friends or doing something else, I was writing code into MS Notepad, relying on compiler feedback to debug, and learning the basics of memory allocation, pointers, flow control, subroutines, and more.

After learning C, I taught myself HTML and CSS, and Blender 3D. Then, when I was 16, I managed to get a tech internship at State Farm, the insurance company. I thought I was pretty computer-savvy, and would have a pretty easy time at State Farm. However, somebody in Human Resources decided that C, my main skill, was about the same as C++, and C++ was about the same as Java. BAM! I got a big reality check when I started working with Java and realized I knew nothing about Object Oriented Programming concepts!

The State Farm internship was also my first exposure to Agile, specifically Scrum style Agile. I returned to state farm in 2016, then after the summer ended I headed off to college!

College Years

When I first started college, I wasn't sure exactly what I wanted to do. Obviously I had the programming experience from two summers of work, plus countless hours of doing it as a hobby. However, I was also very interested in Physics, and I decided to pursue that. After my freshman year, however, I realized that I enjoyed learning physics, but I couldn't see myself going to grad school for it, or getting a job as an engineer.

At the beginning of my sophomore year, I switched majors from physics to computer science. Because there was so much overlap between the programs (math, lab science classes, etc) I managed to graduate in four years with not too much extra effort, and I got two minors: physics and math.

Also my sophomore year, I became a Resident Assistant for university housing, a position I have continued in for four years now! There are many different dorm buildings and even an apartment complex on campus, and I have returned to a different location every year I've worked as an RA. My personal favorite is the apartment complex, Cougar Village. Although its a little further from campus, CV has a much more independent feel, and is very pretty in the autumn.

Work Experience

Being a resident assistant and a full time student kept my busy during the schoolyear, but over the summers, I managed to get lucky and snag a couple internships with State Farm Mutual Insurance Company in Bloomington, IL (Right next to my home town!). I thought it would be the same as the internships I worked in high school, but once again, state farm gave me a challenge. Their college program was harder, and also included a mandatory speech program. However, this time I was much better prepared for the challenge, and gained a lot of new skills, including AWS, Kubernetes, Spring Tool Suite, React and Angular JS, and Scrum Agile!

Graduate School

After graduating (Summa Cum Laude) with my bachelors in computer science, I decided to go back to SIUE for graduate school. SIUE offers an accelerated program where I took grad-level classes during my undergrad senior year, and those got counted toward both my bachelors and my masters, allowing me to cut a semester off my masters program. That's where I am now, and while it's certainly not easy, I am enjoying the challenging but rewarding work I am doing.
